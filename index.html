Air pollution could be reduced by incentivizing local government officials to control crop burning (2025)
Nature: Policy Brief. With Gemma Dipoppa.
Abstract & Materials
PDF Journal Link

Crop-residue burning in South Asia decreases by up to 14.5% when smoke from fires would affect bureaucrats’ home districts. Bureaucratic action to punish farmers deters future fires by a further 13%.

Political Responsiveness, Information Provision, and Capacity Gaps (Forthcoming)
Comparative Political Studies. With Miriam Golden and Luke Sonnet.
Abstract & Materials
PDF JPAL Summary IGC Report

We investigate whether lack of information is one reason politicians may be unresponsive to voter preferences. Using a randomized control trial with senior politicians in Pakistan, we find that even eager and informed politicians may be unable to respond due to institutional capacity constraints.

Can Party Elites Shape the Rank-and-File? Evidence from a Recruitment Experiment in India (2025)
American Political Science Review. With Durgesh Pathak, Sarah Thompson, and Aliz Toth.
Abstract & Materials
PDF Political Science Now Summary

We analyze a field experiment conducted by an Indian party that randomized recruitment messages. Contrary to concerns that parties can only attract a few poor-quality volunteers, we show that elite efforts can shape the rank-and-file.

Publications

Political Responsiveness, Information Provision, and Capacity Gaps (Forthcoming)

Comparative Political Studies With Miriam Golden and Luke Sonnet.
PDF

Abstract: We investigate whether lack of information is one reason politicians may be unresponsive to voter preferences. Using a randomized control trial with senior politicians in Pakistan, we find that even eager and informed politicians may be unable to respond due to institutional capacity constraints.

Golden, M., Gulzar, S., & Sonnet, L. (Forthcoming). Political Responsiveness, Information Provision, and Capacity Gaps. Comparative Political Studies.
Can Party Elites Shape the Rank-and-File? Evidence from a Recruitment Experiment in India (2025)

American Political Science Review With Durgesh Pathak, Sarah Thompson, and Aliz Toth.
PDF

Abstract: We analyze a field experiment conducted by an Indian party that randomized recruitment messages. Contrary to concerns that parties can only attract a few poor-quality volunteers, we show that elite efforts can shape the rank-and-file.

Gulzar, S., Pathak, D., Thompson, S., & Toth, A. (2025). Can Party Elites Shape the Rank-and-File? American Political Science Review.
Personalities and Public Sector Performance: Evidence from a Health Experiment in Pakistan (2025)

Economic Development and Cultural Change With Michael Callen, Ali Hasanain, Muhammad Yasir Khan, and Arman Rezaee.
PDF

Abstract: This paper provides evidence that the personalities of policymakers matter for policy. Doctors with higher Big Five scores attend work more and falsify inspection reports less.

Callen, M., Gulzar, S., Hasanain, A., Khan, M. Y., & Rezaee, A. (2025). Personalities and Public Sector Performance. Economic Development and Cultural Change.
